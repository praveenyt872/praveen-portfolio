Project Abstract

Development of a Self-Regenerating Solar Magneto-Photocatalytic Biochar Nanocomposite for Integrated Wastewater Treatment and Hydrogen Production

Industrial wastewater (IWW) containing persistent organic pollutants (OPs), synthetic dyes, pharmaceutical residues, and toxic heavy metals (HMs) represents a major environmental challenge. Conventional wastewater treatment (CWT) technologies often rely on multi-step processes that increase operational costs and energy consumption while failing to achieve complete pollutant removal.

In this study, a solar-driven magneto-photocatalytic biochar nanocomposite (MPBN) is proposed for simultaneous wastewater remediation and hydrogen (H₂) generation. The multifunctional catalyst consists of biochar (BC) derived from agricultural biomass as a porous support integrated with magnetic iron oxide nanoparticles (Fe₃O₄ MNPs), titanium dioxide (TiO₂) semiconductor photocatalyst, and graphene-based conductive layers (G) to enhance electron transport and catalytic efficiency.

The BC matrix provides a high-surface-area adsorption platform for capturing HMs and OPs through surface interactions, while TiO₂ nanoparticles act as the primary photocatalyst under solar irradiation (SI). Upon light activation, electron–hole pairs are generated, producing reactive oxygen species (ROS) such as hydroxyl radicals (•OH) and superoxide radicals (O₂•⁻), which degrade organic contaminants through photocatalysis (PC).

Simultaneously, photoexcited electrons drive the hydrogen evolution reaction (HER), enabling sustainable H₂ generation. The incorporation of Fe₃O₄ nanoparticles enables rapid catalyst recovery via magnetic separation (MS), improving catalyst reusability and process sustainability. The study evaluates catalyst synthesis (CS), structural characterization, adsorption capacity, pollutant degradation efficiency (PDE), hydrogen production performance (HPP), and long-term catalyst stability. The proposed hybrid system offers a promising integrated strategy for coupling environmental remediation with renewable energy production.
